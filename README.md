Project Overview
This repository contains the econometric framework and longitudinal data for an audit of the Merchant Bank Paradox across seven Anglophone African nations. The study identifies a "Functional Mutation" in the credit channel where commercial banks retreat from private sector lending into sovereign debt, triggering systemic labor market fragmentation.
Core Theoretical Framework
Labor Precariousness Index (LPI): A novel metric developed to quantify the human cost of credit decay.
Institutional Cognitive Atrophy (ICA): Analyzes how high banking sector turnover hollowing out the institutional memory required for SME risk assessment.
Macro-Prudential Human Buffer (MPHB): A policy roadmap for re-anchoring the monetary transmission mechanism toward formal labor absorption.
The 7-Country Panel
The research audits 25 years of data stratified by institutional depth:
The WAMZ Quartet: Nigeria, Ghana, Sierra Leone, Liberia.
Continental Benchmarks: South Africa, Kenya, Zimbabwe.
Empirical Results: Structural Break Intensity (MSD)
Intensity is measured as the Mean Squared Deviation (MSD) Ratio, quantifying the structural "distance" between the pre-shock equilibrium and the post-break volatility.

How to Reproduce
Clone the repository to your local environment.
Ensure pandas, numpy, and requests are installed.
Run python 7Script.py to regenerate the audit from live World Bank data.
